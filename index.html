The frame

60 / 30 / 10.

The Eduba read on most stacks. Sixty percent of the work is traditional code and databases. Thirty percent is rule-based logic. Ten percent is a genuine AI problem. Teams that skip the distinction burn budget putting language models where a Postgres query would do the job faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees.

60%
Traditional Code
Code and database work. Postgres, services, ordinary engineering discipline.
30%
Rule-based Logic
Deterministic decision trees, policy engines, audit-friendly paths.
10%
Actual AI
Where language models earn their seat. Everywhere else, keep them on rails.

For a security-native company, the stakes on that mistake are higher. An LLM in the wrong place becomes an attack surface, a compliance burden, or a leaked training signal. The frame we would bring to your stack is where, inside the enterprise layer, orchestration actually earns its seat, and where the answer is "keep this on rails."
